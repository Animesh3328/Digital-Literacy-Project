Stay Safe Online: Checklist for College Students (India)

 Use strong, unique passwords
Create different passwords for each account and use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Consider a password manager.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Turn on 2FA for email, social media, and banking apps to add an extra layer of security.

Beware of phishing messages
Do not click on suspicious links in emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages claiming to be from banks, colleges, or companies. Always verify the sender.

Never share personal or OTP details
Banks and official institutions never ask for OTPs, passwords, or PINs over calls or messages.

UPI Safety: Never enter PIN to receive money
Remember: you only enter your UPI PIN to send money, not to receive it. “Collect requests” should be verified carefully.

UPI Safety: Avoid unknown QR codes and payment requests
Do not scan QR codes or approve payment requests from unknown people, even if they claim to send you money.

Use secure Wi-Fi networks
Avoid logging into sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi. Use mobile data or a trusted network for banking and payments.

Download apps only from official stores
Install apps only from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid third-party APK files.

Log out from shared devices
Always log out of accounts when using college computers, cyber cafés, or friends’ devices.

Keep your device updated
Regularly update your phone and apps to fix security vulnerabilities.



Report Cybercrime Immediately:
National Cyber Crime Portal: [https://www.cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)
Helpline Number:1930

Quick reporting can help stop fraud and increase the chances of recovering lost money.
